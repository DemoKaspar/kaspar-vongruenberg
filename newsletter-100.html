Newsletter Issue #100

100 issues! Plus: the state of platform engineering in 2024

Hey {NAME},

We made it to 100 issues! 🎉

When I started this newsletter, platform engineering was barely a recognized discipline. Today, it's one of the fastest-growing areas in software engineering. Incredible to see how far we've come.

For issue #100, I wanted to share what I'm seeing across the industry right now—the trends, the challenges, and where I think we're headed.

What's Changed Since Issue #1

Platform engineering is mainstream. Every major tech company now has dedicated platform teams. The conversation has shifted from "What is platform engineering?" to "How do we do it well?"

Tool consolidation is happening. The endless proliferation of DevOps tools is finally stabilizing. Organizations are choosing integrated platforms over point solutions.

Developer experience is the new competitive advantage. Companies that can help their developers ship faster are winning in the market.

What I'm Seeing Right Now

The Good

  • More organizations are treating platforms as products
  • Platform teams are getting better at measuring developer productivity
  • Security is being built into platforms from day one
  • Cost consciousness is driving smarter platform decisions

The Challenging

  • Many teams are still building platforms for themselves, not their users
  • Platform sprawl is becoming the new tool sprawl
  • Skills shortage is real—good platform engineers are hard to find
  • Organizations underestimate the change management required

Predictions for 2025

AI will reshape platform engineering. Not just AI-assisted development, but AI managing platforms themselves. Auto-scaling, self-healing infrastructure, intelligent cost optimization.

Platform standardization will accelerate. Industry standards for platform interfaces will emerge. We'll see more interoperability between platform tools.

The "platform-as-a-service" market will mature. More organizations will buy rather than build their platforms. The focus will shift from infrastructure to business logic.

Thank You

This newsletter exists because thousands of you read it, share it, and reply with your thoughts. The platform engineering community is one of the most collaborative and generous I've encountered in tech.

Special thanks to everyone who's shared their platform stories, participated in surveys, and helped shape the direction of this newsletter. You make it better every week.

Here's to the next 100 issues!

With gratitude,

Kaspar
