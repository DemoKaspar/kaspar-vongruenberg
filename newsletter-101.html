Newsletter Issue #101

Platform maturity levels: where does your organization stand?

Hey {NAME},

Last week I was at a CTO roundtable where every single leader was asking the same question in different ways: "How do I know if our platform engineering efforts are actually working?"

Great question. The problem is that "platform maturity" means different things to different people. So I've been working on a simple framework to help organizations understand where they stand.

The Five Levels of Platform Maturity

Level 0: Artisanal Development

What it looks like: Every team builds and deploys however they want. Kubernetes YAML scattered everywhere. Each service has its own custom CI/CD pipeline.

Pain points: Long deployment times, frequent outages, developers spending 60% of time on infrastructure.

Success metric: "It works on my machine"

Level 1: Basic Automation

What it looks like: Shared CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure as code, maybe a service mesh. Teams still manage their own deployments but with some consistency.

Pain points: Configuration drift, security vulnerabilities, cognitive overhead still high.

Success metric: Deployment success rate

Level 2: Self-Service Platform

What it looks like: Developers can provision infrastructure and deploy applications through APIs or web interfaces. Golden paths exist for common patterns.

Pain points: Platform adoption is inconsistent, some teams still go around the platform.

Success metric: Platform adoption rate

Level 3: Intelligent Platform

What it looks like: Platform provides recommendations, auto-scaling, cost optimization. Policy enforcement is automated. Observability is built-in.

Pain points: Platform complexity is increasing, need specialized platform teams.

Success metric: Developer velocity and satisfaction

Level 4: Autonomous Platform

What it looks like: Platform manages itself. Self-healing infrastructure, predictive scaling, automated compliance. Developers focus purely on business logic.

Pain points: High complexity to maintain, requires significant investment.

Success metric: Business outcome velocity

Where Should You Be?

Here's the thing: you don't need to be at Level 4. Most successful organizations operate at Level 2 or 3. The key is matching your platform maturity to your organizational needs:

  • Startup with 10 developers: Level 1 is probably perfect
  • Scale-up with 50 developers: Level 2 will solve most problems
  • Enterprise with 500+ developers: Level 3 becomes essential
  • Tech company with thousands of developers: Level 4 might be worth the investment

The Most Important Question

It's not "What level are we?" It's "Are we solving the right problems for our stage?"

I've seen Level 0 startups ship faster than Level 3 enterprises because they're optimized for their context.

Focus on the problems, not the sophistication.
