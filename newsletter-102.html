Newsletter Issue #102

The hardest part about platforms? Getting people to use them

Hey {NAME},

I just finished a two-day workshop with a Fortune 500 company. They've built an impressive internal developer platform—excellent tooling, solid architecture, thoughtful abstractions. But adoption is stuck at 30%.

"We built it, but they're not coming," the platform lead told me during a coffee break.

Sound familiar? This is the most common challenge I see. Technical execution is rarely the issue. The real problem is that we're asking people to change how they work, and change is hard.

Why Developers Resist Platforms

  • Fear of losing control: "What if the platform can't do what I need?"
  • Learning curve: "I already know how to deploy with kubectl"
  • Trust issues: "What if the platform team breaks something?"
  • Identity: "I'm a backend engineer, not a platform consumer"

These aren't technical problems. They're human problems.

What Actually Works

After working with dozens of platform teams, here's what I've learned actually drives adoption:

1. Start with the willing

Don't try to convert everyone at once. Find teams that are genuinely excited about the platform and make them wildly successful. Success is contagious.

2. Make the old way harder than the new way

Gentle nudges don't work. Create meaningful incentives. One company I worked with required manual deployments to go through a lengthy approval process, while platform deployments were instant.

3. Embed with teams

The most successful platform teams I know embed engineers with application teams for 1-2 sprints. Not to build features, but to understand real workflows and remove friction.

4. Celebrate platform users publicly

Recognition matters. Highlight teams that use the platform in all-hands meetings. Show their velocity improvements, their reduced toil, their focus on business logic.

The Migration Mindset

Stop thinking of platform adoption as a technical rollout. Think of it as a migration project with a clear timeline, milestones, and support structure.

The companies with 90%+ adoption don't have better platforms. They have better change management.

Your platform might be technically perfect, but if people aren't using it, it's just expensive infrastructure.

Focus on the humans first. The technology will follow.

Talk soon,

Kaspar
