Newsletter Issue #103

Want to embed AI in the SDLC? Platform Engineering is inevitable

Hey {NAME},

Every week I get a couple of emails asking me about how Platform Engineering helps with AI adoption. There are multiple dimensions to this question but today I want to share something specific: if an organization wants to integrate AI (LLMs, Agents, etc.) into the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC), Platform Engineering becomes inevitable. Let me walk you through the why.

The Reality of AI in SDLC

When teams start integrating AI into their development workflows, they're not just adding a new tool—they're introducing a new class of dependencies with unique requirements:

  • 💸 AI services are expensive and usage needs to be managed
  • 🔒 Code and data flowing through AI services creates new security concerns
  • 🎯 Different teams need different AI capabilities (code generation, testing, documentation, etc.)
  • 📏 AI outputs need to be evaluated and quality-assured
  • 🏛️ Compliance and governance become critical when code is AI-assisted

Why Individual Teams Can't Handle This Alone

I've seen what happens when organizations let individual teams figure out AI integration on their own:

  • Teams end up with 15 different AI tools, each with different security postures
  • No visibility into AI usage costs until the bill arrives
  • Inconsistent quality standards for AI-generated code
  • Compliance nightmares when auditors ask about AI usage in production code
  • Teams spending more time managing AI toolchains than building features

Platform Engineering as the Solution

This is exactly the kind of complexity that Platform Engineering was designed to solve. Here's what I typically see successful organizations do:

Curated AI Service Catalog

Instead of chaos, provide teams with a curated set of AI services that have been pre-approved for security, cost-effectiveness, and compliance. Think of it as "AI as a Service" within your platform.

Integrated AI Workflows

Build AI capabilities directly into your golden paths. For example, automatic code review suggestions, test generation, or documentation updates that happen seamlessly within the existing development workflow.

Governance and Observability

Centralized monitoring of AI usage, cost allocation, quality metrics, and compliance reporting. Teams get the benefits without the operational overhead.

The Inevitable Conclusion

Organizations that want to seriously adopt AI in their SDLC face a choice: either let chaos reign as teams build their own AI solutions, or provide a platform that makes AI adoption scalable, secure, and governed.

The companies I see succeeding with AI in development aren't the ones with the most AI tools—they're the ones that have made AI capabilities easily consumable through their internal platform.

Platform Engineering isn't just helpful for AI adoption; it's inevitable if you want to do it right.

Talk soon,

Kaspar
