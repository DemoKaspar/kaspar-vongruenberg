Newsletter Issue #106

How much should we AI-ify the engineering process already?

Hey {NAME},

I spent the last few days building an end to end Internal Developer Platform using Cortex, AWS (VMs this time!), Humanitec and a couple other tools (here's the recording). I already used Claude Code as a pair programming assistant and pretty much all AI features that the tools I used gave to me. The Cortex MCP server is truly great. For instance, I used the AI score cards and while building the setup Claude was a fantastic co-worker.

Then on Saturday I organized a brunch for a dear friend of mine who turned 50. Half the table were engineers and we were chit chatting about the engineering setups we were all using. Half of them were already at a point where the engineering workflow was AI-coding first, human coding second. In the direction of:

The New AI-First Workflow

  • Developers write the tickets and describe the intent in detail.
  • Pair Programming Assistants would pick up and do a first pass on the code. (Claude was the favourite but used less frequently because of the cost, Codex is frontrunner from a cost-benefit perspective).
  • The Output gets passed to up to 3 additional coding assistants for review and then surfaced to the human operator who then reads the code and does eventual alterations.

Those weren't hobby teams that just stumbled out of the JS bootcamp in Berlin downtown, they were serious backend guys that know their stuff. They all report how much time they are spending on the system prompt, how they are spending wild amounts on CI runs suddenly, and how they are going more and more into a mono-repo direction.

But first and foremost they are reporting a 30% increase in productivity. Despite what the studies might say.

The Reality Check

Are you someone seeing success? Or failure? Or just have really strong opinions. I'm looking to talk to more people that have really advanced AI fueled engineering setups and I'm also looking at people that don't buy the hype and think it's nonsense.

Have a strong opinion? Hit reply to this mail. And yes, I do read all replies myself!

Kaspar
