Newsletter Issue #107

How do you build roadmaps for platforms?

Hey {NAME},

I have had the pleasure of running a few larger scale trainings at Platform Engineering teams further along in their journey. Teams that are maybe in year 3-5 of their platform. And a lot of their questions were about how to build and prioritize roadmaps if your platform is running already. This is a big field but here is the approach I am personally using, maybe it helps!

First, congratulations that you are thinking about a roadmap for your platform. Looks like you are treating it as a product and that is the first step for success in Platform Engineering! For the sake of this conversation let's assume you are in year 3 of your platform journey, have built a Minimum Viable Platform and adoption is going well. How do you go from here and improve The good news is that you can basically follow a decision tree, let me walk you through it:

Which Platform Are We Talking About?

That's a relevant question because you will likely not have one roadmap to rule them all. Pretty much every engineering team with 200 devs + has at least 3 platforms: one for app development, one for hosting third party stuff and one for ML/AI; For the sake of this example lets say we are building a roadmap for the app devs!

Talk to Your Users

Zooming into the Application Development side of things, lets screen the teams that are using your platforms on a daily basis and that are on the most common tech stack (did somebody say EKS?). Single out a team that likes your platform already and doesn't send a lot of support requests, do not go to the loudest voices, they will skew your judgement, go to the silent majority. Now TALK TO THEM! You heard me right. DO NOT guess what they would want next from your platform. Nobody cares about your judgement, everybody c about reality.

The problem is if you go to them and ask them what they think they need next from the platform they won't actually know. Because they are business blind and have little clue how great life is on the other side. Now go figure out (If you haven't done it already yet) what value streams exist alongside the meta stream "I create an application - to - I sunset an application". An example of a value stream would be "I need to spin up an experimental POC before I start building new services fast". I will write a lot more about this in the future, knowing your value streams are insanely important. Log it for all eternity.

Map User Journeys and Paths

Next you take a single value stream from intent to outcome (for the business!!) and you contemplate with your users at what point they have any interaction with your platform. For instance during the value stream "creating POC" the user might want to have an ephemeral environment to test their POC service. Those intents and touch points with your platforms are what I call "path". You should aim to list all these relevant paths.

Once you have listed all relevant paths you want to understand what paths - if offered and exposed by your platform, would bring the most value to the user and the organization. I like to figure this out with a mix of simply asking people (beware of biases) and napkin math. Let's think about previous example of adding a path to help users rapidly create ephemeral environments. Napkin math would tell us that today users are waiting 30min a week for blocked staging environments. Assume you serve 500 developers, that is the equivalent of approx 650k of cost for the org a year or the equivalent of 6 full time positions. I really encourage you to do these little calculations and just identify what outcome the creation of each path would realize.

Prioritize and Build

Once you are done with that go ahead and rank them by impact and user desire (maybe you want to give your users a treat but it doesn't move the needle for the org, that's legit too!). Take your force t of paths and dive deep on the request format, the interface(s) through which the path would be requested and lastly (not earlier) think about how to design the capability of this path and how to policy check its outcome.

Pick the 4-5 most impactful paths from your list and build quick test versions of your path, essentially minimum viable versions to check feasibility before investing into production versions. Run a quick test with your devs. Works? Perfect, build the production version and roll it out, assess and pick the next set of paths.

Note that we are still focussing on a single team (though do continue to make sure this is representative of the broader audience). I always advise to go from small scale focus to big scale roll-out in waves.

Scale Gradually
