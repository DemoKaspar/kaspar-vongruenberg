Newsletter Issue #40

The first ever, 100% virtual PlatformCon!

Hey {NAME},

Issue #40 and what a milestone to celebrate - we're announcing the first ever PlatformCon! A conference dedicated entirely to Platform Engineering, and it's going to be 100% virtual and 100% free.

I couldn't be more excited about this. Platform Engineering has grown from a niche practice to a movement that's transforming how we build and operate software. It deserves its own conference, its own community, and its own space to share learnings.

Why PlatformCon?

Over the past few months, I've been talking to platform engineers around the world, and the same theme keeps coming up: isolation. So many teams are building platforms in relative isolation, solving similar problems, making similar mistakes, and discovering similar solutions.

There's incredible innovation happening in platform engineering, but it's scattered across organizations. Teams at Netflix are solving problems that teams at Spotify faced two years ago. Teams at startups are reinventing solutions that enterprises have already perfected.

We need a way to share knowledge, patterns, and hard-won lessons across the entire community. That's what PlatformCon is for.

What to Expect

PlatformCon isn't going to be another generic DevOps conference with a few platform talks thrown in. This is going to be laser-focused on platform engineering:

Real case studies from teams building platforms at scale. Not vendor pitches, but actual experiences - what worked, what didn't, what they'd do differently.

Technical deep dives into platform architecture patterns, tooling decisions, and implementation strategies.

Organizational insights about how to build platform teams, measure success, and drive adoption across engineering organizations.

Community building - connecting platform engineers with each other to share experiences and learn from each other.

Why Virtual?

We made the decision to make PlatformCon 100% virtual for several reasons:

Accessibility: Platform engineers are scattered around the globe. A virtual event means we can bring together practitioners from every timezone and every continent.

Inclusivity: Not everyone can afford to travel to conferences, take time off work, or navigate visa requirements. Virtual removes these barriers.

Reach: We can accommodate thousands of participants instead of hundreds, making this truly a community-scale event.

Environmental impact: No travel means no carbon footprint. As platform engineers, we should be thinking about the environmental impact of our choices.

Plus, after two years of getting really good at virtual events, we know how to make them engaging and interactive.

The Speaker Lineup

We're assembling an incredible lineup of speakers from organizations that are leading the way in platform engineering:

  • Platform teams from major tech companies sharing their architecture decisions and lessons learned
  • Startup platform engineers talking about building platforms with limited resources
  • Enterprise platform teams discussing governance, compliance, and scaling challenges
  • Tool builders sharing insights about the future of platform engineering technology

The focus is on practitioners sharing real experiences, not vendors pitching products.

Community-Driven Content

This isn't going to be a typical top-down conference. We're building PlatformCon with and for the community:

Open call for speakers: We want to hear from platform engineers at every level, at every type of organization. If you're building platforms, we want to hear your story.

Community voting: The community will help decide which talks get selected and which topics get the most focus.

Interactive sessions: We're planning workshops, roundtables, and discussion sessions alongside traditional talks.

Lightning talks: Short, focused presentations about specific platform engineering challenges and solutions.

Building the Platform Engineering Community

PlatformCon is about more than just a single event - it's about building a lasting community around platform engineering. We're planning:

Year-round content: Monthly virtual meetups, case study sharing, and community discussions.

Regional chapters: Local meetups in major cities to complement the global virtual events.

Knowledge sharing: A repository of platform engineering patterns, tools, and best practices contributed by the community.

Mentorship programs: Connecting experienced platform engineers with teams just getting started.

Get Involved

Want to be part of PlatformCon? Here's how you can get involved:

Submit a talk: We're looking for talks about platform engineering experiences, technical deep dives, and organizational insights. No vendor pitches, please - this is about sharing genuine learnings.

Spread the word: Tell other platform engineers in your network about PlatformCon. The bigger the community, the better the content and connections.

Join the planning: We're looking for volunteers to help with content curation, community building, and event logistics.

Attend and participate: Registration is free, but we're hoping for active participation in discussions, Q&A sessions, and community building.

The Future of Platform Engineering

Platform Engineering is at an inflection point. It's moving from an emergent practice to an established discipline. We're seeing dedicated platform teams at major companies, platform engineering job titles, and platform engineering tools and vendors.

PlatformCon is our chance to shape what this discipline becomes. To establish best practices, share knowledge, and build the community that will carry platform engineering forward.

I can't wait to see what we build together.

Save the date - more details coming soon!

Kaspar
