Newsletter Issue #80

CNCF accepted Score & we'll double down on open source

Hey {NAME},

BIG NEWS! The Cloud Native Computing Foundation (CNCF) has officially accepted Score as a Sandbox project! 🎉

This is a huge milestone for us and for the platform engineering community. Score is now part of the CNCF landscape alongside projects like Kubernetes, Prometheus, and Envoy.

What does this mean?

First, it's a validation that the problem Score solves - workload specification that's environment-agnostic - is real and important enough for the CNCF to get behind it.

Second, it means Score will benefit from the CNCF's governance, security practices, and community processes. The project becomes more stable and trustworthy as a result.

Third, and most importantly for you as practitioners, it means Score will have better longevity and community support. You can bet on it as a foundational piece of your platform without worrying about it being abandoned or having its direction change arbitrarily.

Why Score matters for platform engineering

If you've been following this newsletter, you know I'm obsessed with the separation of concerns between application developers and platform teams. Score is the interface that makes this separation possible.

With Score, developers can describe their workload requirements (I need a database, I need a cache, I need to expose these ports) without getting into the specifics of how those requirements are met in each environment.

Platform teams can then implement these requirements differently in dev, staging, and production - using different database sizes, different caching solutions, different ingress configurations - without the developers having to know or care about these details.

This is exactly the kind of abstraction that makes platform engineering work at scale.

Our commitment to open source

Score's acceptance into CNCF is also us putting our money where our mouth is on open source. We're doubling down on our commitment to building in the open and contributing to the community.

Here's what this means concretely:

  • Score development will be completely transparent and community-driven
  • All roadmap decisions will be made in the open
  • We'll be investing more engineering time into Score itself, not just our commercial implementation
  • We'll be working to get more companies and projects to adopt Score as their workload specification

What's next?

Now that Score is in CNCF, we're going to be pushing hard to get broader adoption. We're already working with several other platform engineering tools to add Score support.

We're also going to be investing heavily in making Score more powerful while keeping it simple. Think better validation, better extensibility, better tooling around it.

If you haven't tried Score yet, now is a great time to give it a spin. And if you're already using it, we'd love your feedback on what you'd like to see next.

This is a big step forward for platform engineering standardization. I'm excited to see where the community takes Score from here!

Cheers,
Kaspar
