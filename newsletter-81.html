Newsletter Issue #81

Platform Engineering Certification

Hey {NAME},

I think one thing that is missing in our Platform Engineering discussion is proper training and certification. The issue is that there is still little structure as to what exactly people need to know to do Platform Engineering properly.

This creates two problems: First, how do I as a platform engineering practitioner know that I'm doing things correctly, am following best practices, and have mastered the skillsets I need to master? Second, how do I as a hiring manager, assess whether candidates know what they are talking about, and hire the right people?

The market is currently dealing with this by referencing "Kubernetes skills" which works to some degree because it's pretty binary: someone either knows Kubernetes or they don't. But that's obviously an imperfect proxy.

A good Platform Engineering certification should cover technical skills like:

  • Container orchestration and Kubernetes
  • Infrastructure as Code (Terraform, Pulumi, etc.)
  • CI/CD pipeline design and implementation
  • GitOps principles and tooling
  • Observability and monitoring
  • Security and policy as code
  • Service mesh and networking

But just as importantly, it should cover the soft skills and organizational aspects:

  • Platform product management
  • Developer experience design
  • Stakeholder management
  • Documentation and self-service enablement
  • Adoption strategies and change management
  • Measuring platform success and ROI

The technical skills are what most people focus on, but in my experience, the organizational and product management skills are what separate successful platform teams from those that struggle.

What would such a certification look like in practice?

I imagine it would be a combination of:

  • Technical hands-on labs
  • Case study analysis
  • Platform design exercises
  • ROI and metrics evaluation

The hands-on component is crucial - you can't learn platform engineering just from theory. But the strategic component is equally important because platform engineering is ultimately about enabling teams and driving business outcomes.

Who should offer such certification?

This is where it gets tricky. The cloud providers each want to certify people on their specific platforms, but platform engineering is fundamentally about abstraction and vendor neutrality. A good certification should be cloud-agnostic while still being practically applicable.

I think the ideal would be for industry bodies like CNCF to develop certification programs, with various training providers offering courses to prepare for the certification.

At Humanitec, we're exploring how we might contribute to this. Not by creating our own certification (that would be too self-serving) but by contributing to industry-wide standards and potentially offering training that prepares people for whatever industry-standard certification emerges.

What do you think? Would you be interested in Platform Engineering certification? What skills and knowledge areas do you think should be covered?

Let me know your thoughts!

Kaspar
