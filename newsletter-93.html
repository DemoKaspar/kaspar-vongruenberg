Newsletter Issue #93

How to handle platform migrations (without breaking everything)

Hey {NAME},

Platform migrations are some of the scariest projects in software engineering. You're changing the infrastructure that every team depends on, often while they're still shipping features.

I've helped dozens of organizations migrate platforms—from monoliths to microservices, from VMs to containers, from on-prem to cloud, from custom tools to standardized platforms.

Here's how to do it without breaking everything (and everyone's trust in your platform team).

Why Platform Migrations Are So Risky

Platform migrations are uniquely challenging because:

  • Scale: You're affecting every application and team
  • Dependencies: Applications depend on platform behaviors they may not even know about
  • Urgency: Teams want to keep shipping while you're migrating
  • Expertise: You're asking every team to learn new tools and patterns
  • Risk: A mistake affects everyone simultaneously

The Strangler Fig Pattern for Platforms

The best platform migrations use the "Strangler Fig" pattern—gradually replacing the old platform with the new one, piece by piece.

Phase 1: Parallel Systems

Build the new platform alongside the old one. Don't migrate any workloads yet. Focus on:

  • Getting the new platform to feature parity with the critical paths
  • Building migration tooling
  • Training your platform team on the new system
  • Creating comprehensive documentation

Phase 2: Pilot Migrations

Choose 2-3 volunteer teams with simple, non-critical applications. Focus on:

  • Learning what migration pain points you missed
  • Refining your migration process
  • Building confidence in the new platform
  • Creating migration success stories

Phase 3: Systematic Migration

Migrate applications in waves, starting with the easiest and least risky:

  • New applications (always start on the new platform)
  • Development environments first, then production
  • Simple applications before complex ones
  • Teams that are excited about the change

Phase 4: Forced Migration

Eventually, you'll need to set a sunset date for the old platform and move everyone off:

  • Give teams 6-12 months notice
  • Provide dedicated migration support
  • Make the old platform increasingly expensive/inconvenient
  • Have an escalation process for truly stuck teams

Migration Success Principles

1. Make the new thing better, not just different

Don't just replicate old platform functionality. Solve real pain points that teams have with the current platform.

2. Provide migration tools, not migration instructions

Build automated migration tools rather than asking teams to manually recreate their configurations.

3. Support both platforms during transition

Don't let the old platform rot while you build the new one. Teams stuck on the old platform will hate you.

4. Be transparent about timeline and process

Regular updates, clear milestones, and honest communication about blockers and delays.

5. Provide hands-on migration support

Don't just throw documentation over the fence. Embed with teams, pair on migrations, and solve problems together.

Common Migration Mistakes

The "big bang" approach

Trying to migrate everything at once. This maximizes risk and makes rollback impossible.

Building the new platform in isolation

Not involving application teams in the design process, then wondering why adoption is low.

Underestimating migration effort

Assuming that migration will be quick because "it's just moving from A to B."

Neglecting the change management aspect

Focusing only on technical migration and ignoring the human side of change.

No rollback plan

Not having a clear plan for what to do if the migration goes wrong.

Managing Migration Communication

Create a migration webpage

One source of truth for migration status, timelines, resources, and FAQs.

Regular migration newsletters

Weekly or bi-weekly updates on migration progress, upcoming deadlines, and success stories.

Migration office hours

Regular sessions where teams can ask questions and get help with their migrations.

Success story showcases

Highlight teams that have successfully migrated and the benefits they're seeing.

Technical Migration Tips

Build compatibility layers

Create adapters that let applications use old APIs while running on new infrastructure.

Use feature flags for platform features

Let teams gradually adopt new platform capabilities rather than all at once.

Invest heavily in monitoring and rollback

You need to quickly detect when migrations go wrong and be able to rollback fast.

Automate everything you can

Configuration migration, testing, deployment—anything that can be automated should be.

When Migrations Go Wrong

Sometimes migrations fail. When they do:

  • Communicate immediately: Tell affected teams what happened and what you're doing about it
  • Rollback fast: Don't try to fix forward if rollback is an option
  • Do a blameless post-mortem: Learn from what went wrong
  • Adjust your process: Fix the migration process before continuing
  • Rebuild trust: Be extra careful and communicative with subsequent migrations

The Long View

Platform migrations typically take 12-24 months from start to finish. That's normal and expected.

Don't rush. A slow, successful migration is better than a fast, broken one.

Focus on making the new platform so much better that teams want to migrate, not just making the old platform so bad they have to.
