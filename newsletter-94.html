Newsletter Issue #94

Platform observability: what to measure and why

Hey {NAME},

A platform team reached out last week with a question that's surprisingly common: "We built an internal developer platform, but we don't know if it's working. What should we be measuring?"

Good platform observability isn't just about uptime and response times. You need to understand how your platform affects developer productivity, business outcomes, and organizational efficiency.

Here's what to measure and why.

The Three Layers of Platform Metrics

Layer 1: Platform Health Metrics

These tell you if your platform is functioning:

  • Uptime/Availability: Is your platform available when developers need it?
  • Response times: How fast do platform APIs respond?
  • Error rates: What percentage of platform operations fail?
  • Resource utilization: Are you over or under-provisioned?

Why these matter: A broken platform creates developer frustration and reduces productivity.

Layer 2: Developer Experience Metrics

These tell you how your platform affects developers:

  • Lead time: How long from code commit to production deployment?
  • Deployment frequency: How often are teams shipping?
  • Time to first deployment: How long does it take new developers to ship their first change?
  • Platform adoption rate: What percentage of eligible teams are using your platform?
  • Self-service success rate: What percentage of requests are handled without human intervention?
  • Developer satisfaction: How do developers rate your platform?

Why these matter: These connect platform functionality to developer productivity.

Layer 3: Business Impact Metrics

These tell you how your platform affects business outcomes:

  • Feature delivery velocity: Are teams shipping features faster?
  • Compliance adherence: Are applications meeting security/compliance requirements?
  • Cost efficiency: Are you reducing infrastructure costs per application?
  • Incident reduction: Are you reducing production incidents?
  • Time to market: How quickly can new products/services be launched?

Why these matter: These justify platform investment to leadership.

The DORA Metrics for Platforms

The DORA metrics (Lead Time, Deployment Frequency, Mean Time to Recovery, Change Failure Rate) are great for measuring platform impact, but you need to measure them correctly:

Lead Time

Don't measure: Just the platform deployment time

Do measure: End-to-end lead time including platform provisioning, deployment, and verification

Deployment Frequency

Don't measure: Just successful deployments

Do measure: Deployment attempts and success rates separately

Mean Time to Recovery

Don't measure: Just platform infrastructure recovery

Do measure: Time to restore service functionality for end users

Change Failure Rate

Don't measure: Just deployment failures

Do measure: Changes that require hotfixes, rollbacks, or cause service degradation

Platform-Specific Metrics to Track

Golden Path Metrics

  • Time to complete each golden path workflow
  • Success rate of golden path completions
  • Percentage of teams using golden paths vs. custom solutions

Support and Onboarding Metrics

  • Number of support tickets per platform user
  • Time to resolution for different types of issues
  • Onboarding completion rates
  • Time from platform access to first successful deployment

Resource Efficiency Metrics

  • Infrastructure cost per application/team
  • Resource utilization rates
  • Waste reduction (unused resources, over-provisioning)

How to Collect These Metrics

Automated instrumentation

Build metrics collection into your platform from day one. Every API call, every workflow, every user action should be logged and measured.

Regular surveys

Quarterly developer satisfaction surveys. Ask specific questions about platform pain points and desired features.

Integration with existing tools

Pull data from your CI/CD systems, issue trackers, and monitoring tools rather than building everything from scratch.

Business metrics integration

Work with product teams to understand how platform improvements correlate with business outcomes.

Common Measurement Mistakes

  • Measuring everything: Too many metrics create noise, not insight
  • Vanity metrics: Measuring things that look good but don't drive decisions
  • Technical metrics only: Missing the business impact story
  • Point-in-time measurements: Not tracking trends over time
  • No action on insights: Collecting data but not using it to improve the platform

Building Your Metrics Dashboard

Your platform metrics dashboard should tell a story:

  1. Executive summary: High-level business impact in 3-4 key metrics
  2. Developer experience: How the platform is serving developers
  3. Platform health: Technical performance and reliability
  4. Trends and insights: What's getting better or worse over time

The Goal: Data-Driven Platform Evolution

The purpose of platform observability isn't just reporting—it's evolution. Your metrics should help you:

  • Prioritize platform improvements
  • Identify adoption barriers
  • Prove business value
  • Spot problems before they become critical

Start with a few key metrics that matter most to your organization, and expand from there. Perfect measurement is the enemy of useful measurement.
