Newsletter Issue #99

How to measure platform ROI (without making everyone angry)

Hey {NAME},

Every platform team eventually faces this moment: leadership wants to see the ROI numbers.

"How much money are we saving with this platform?"

"What's our return on investment?"

"Can you show me the business case?"

The honest answer? Platform ROI is really hard to measure. But here's how to do it without making everyone (including yourself) miserable in the process.

The Wrong Way to Measure Platform ROI

Don't measure:

  • Infrastructure cost savings alone - Platforms often cost more in infrastructure because they enable more usage
  • Time to deploy - Fast deployments don't necessarily mean business value
  • Tickets reduced - Fewer support tickets might mean worse self-service UX
  • Lines of code eliminated - Less code isn't always better

These metrics miss the point and often make platforms look like cost centers.

A Better Approach: The 3-Layer Model

Layer 1: Developer Experience Metrics

Measure how the platform affects developers:

  • Lead time from idea to production
  • Developer satisfaction scores (quarterly surveys)
  • Time spent on undifferentiated work vs. feature development
  • Onboarding time for new developers

Layer 2: Platform Health Metrics

Measure how well the platform itself is working:

  • Platform adoption rate across teams
  • Platform uptime and reliability
  • Self-service success rate (requests handled without human intervention)
  • Mean time to resolution for platform issues

Layer 3: Business Impact Metrics

Connect platform success to business outcomes:

  • Feature release frequency
  • Time to market for new products
  • Engineering team scaling efficiency
  • Compliance adherence

The ROI Story That Actually Works

Instead of trying to calculate a precise dollar amount, tell a story:

"Before our platform, it took 3 weeks to onboard a new developer and 4 days to deploy a simple service. Now it takes 2 days and 10 minutes respectively. This allows our 50-person engineering team to ship 40% more features per quarter. At our current ARR of $10M, each additional feature typically drives 2-5% growth, meaning the platform enables $80K-$200K in additional revenue per quarter."

The Secret: Focus on Enablement, Not Savings

The best platform ROI stories aren't about what you saved—they're about what you enabled.

  • New markets you entered faster
  • Features you shipped that drove revenue
  • Compliance requirements you met without slowing development
  • Engineering talent you attracted and retained

Pro Tips for Your ROI Presentation

  1. Use before/after stories - Show specific examples of how work changed
  2. Let developers tell the story - Their voices carry more weight than yours
  3. Connect to business priorities - Frame platform success in terms of company goals
  4. Acknowledge the investment - Be honest about platform costs and complexity
  5. Show the trajectory - Platforms get more valuable over time as adoption grows

The Bottom Line

Platform ROI isn't about perfect metrics. It's about telling a compelling story that connects developer productivity to business outcomes.

Focus on enablement over efficiency, and let the humans tell the story of how their work changed.

The numbers will follow.

Talk soon,

Kaspar
